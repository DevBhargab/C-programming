Virat Kohli was born on 5th November 1988 in Delhi, into a Punjabi Hindu family. His mother Saroj Kohli was a homemaker, and his father Prem Nath Kohli was a criminal lawyer. Virat showed a passion for cricket at an early age and began playing at just 3 years old.

He joined the West Delhi Cricket Academy at the age of 9 and quickly rose through the ranks, eventually representing Delhi in the Ranji Trophy. His breakthrough came in the U-19 World Cup, leading to his international debut for India in August 2008.

Kohli became the face of Royal Challengers Bangalore (RCB) in the IPL and remains their highest run-scorer. Despite not winning the IPL, he holds multiple records including the most centuries in ODIs. He led India to numerous victories including the 2011 ODI World Cup and 2013 Champions Trophy.

With over 9000 runs in each format and a batting average above 50, Kohli is considered one of the greatest cricketers of all time.
